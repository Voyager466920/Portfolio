PLANTABIT

A serverless to-do list that helps plants, people, and the planet.

Environment Keeper

2023.11 – 2024.05

PLANTABIT is a to-do list designed for the environment. Each task encourages actions that benefit the planet — reducing plastic, planting greenery, choosing eco-friendly transport. More than just organizing tasks, it tries to make daily eco-conscious decisions feel rewarding.

One of the key decisions was to keep it serverless. Servers are invisible to most users, but they consume significant electricity and emit carbon. By running entirely offline with no remote backend, PLANTABIT minimizes its own footprint while encouraging users to reduce theirs.

Design-wise, PLANTABIT was a continuation of what I learned through FEELM. I revisited the Dieter Rams-inspired principles and gave them more clarity, hierarchy, and feedback. I also built on the local database architecture I first explored with Ending Credit, using SQFlite more deliberately to manage data in a structured, efficient way.

But the most important shift was in user experience. I wanted sustainability to feel satisfying. Small touches like confetti animations turn the to-do list into a daily moment of positive reinforcement — rewarding not just task completion, but commitment to the planet.

PLANTABIT shipped on both the App Store and Google Play. It's a personal project born out of concern for our shared future, and a reminder that even the smallest habits, done with intention, can plant the seeds of real change.
