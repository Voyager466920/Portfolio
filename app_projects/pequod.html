Pequod

Why don't we keep our animals alive?

Environmental Animal Saver

2024.06 – 2024.12

Inspired by the provocative, rebellious energy of Liquid Death, Pequod is not your typical sustainability app. It doesn't politely ask you to be eco-friendly—it demands it. And if you don’t act, someone (or something) pays the price.

In Pequod, users are assigned an animal—your digital companion, your responsibility. To keep it alive, you must complete real-world, environmentally conscious tasks: things like reducing waste, biking instead of driving, or conserving water. But unlike PLANTABIT, which gently rewards users for small acts of kindness, Pequod punishes neglect. If you fail to act, the animal suffers. This is eco-guilt gamified—dark, visceral, and urgent.

Technically, Pequod is backed by a server architecture, enabling account-based progress, global leaderboards, and real-time status updates. It diverges from the serverless nature of PLANTABIT, not just in infrastructure, but in philosophy—Pequod is social, aggressive, and consequence-driven.

What truly sets Pequod apart is its integration of AI-powered computer vision. Users are required to prove their eco-actions by taking photos—of reusable shopping bags, planted trees, recycling, etc. These images are then analyzed using a custom-trained image classifier. If your submission passes, you earn time for your animal to live on. If not… well, the clock keeps ticking.

Pequod is about flipping the sustainability narrative. It doesn’t sugarcoat climate change. It doesn’t hand out confetti. It creates pressure, urgency, and emotional weight—because that’s what the crisis deserves. This isn't feel-good greenwashing. It’s digital survival.
