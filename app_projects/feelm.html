FEELM

A movie recommender that listens to how you feel.

Bauhaus Design

2022.02 – 2023.09

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." "Form follows function." These two principles from the Bauhaus movement have shaped much of modern design — and they became the foundation for FEELM. The app connects how you feel with what you watch, trying to answer a simple question: what kind of film suits my mood right now?

Rather than chasing trends or building a competitive product, FEELM was a design study, heavily inspired by the work of Dieter Rams. I focused on clarity, restraint, and usability — translating Rams' "less but better" approach into Flutter, one element at a time.

AI wasn't as mature or accessible as it is today, so FEELM didn't use machine learning. Instead, it ran on a simple numerical recommendation system built around manually assigned emotion tags and film metadata. The trade-off was intentional: keep the experience lightweight and intuitive.

Building a highly structured visual design in Flutter came with its own challenges. Every visual and interactive element had to be hand-crafted, and as design fidelity increased, so did the app's weight and complexity. Performance and scalability became real costs.

Even with those limitations, FEELM shipped on both the App Store and Google Play. It remains a cornerstone of my early exploration of design systems, emotional UX, and human-centered interaction.
