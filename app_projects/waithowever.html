Wait, However

Reinventing the fact-checker.

LLM-based Fact-Checker

2025.02 –

Wait, However is a new kind of fact-checking tool — one that doesn't just confirm or deny what's in an article, but actively identifies what's missing.

Built on large language models, it helps readers confront bias, recognize blind spots, and see the other side of sensitive or complex issues. It surfaces alternative viewpoints, underrepresented angles, and counterarguments the original article may have ignored. The goal isn't to tell users what to believe — it's to empower them to become more critical, democratic consumers of information.

Using it is simple: paste the URL of any article you're reading. Behind the scenes, the AI analyzes the content, contextualizes it against broader knowledge, and returns thoughtful prompts, opposing perspectives, and relevant facts that may shift how you understand the topic.

Wait, However is more than a concept — it's been recognized in competitions at KAIST and other national programs. It's a showcase of how AI can be used responsibly: not just for generation, but for deeper engagement and accountability in the information ecosystem.

Beyond perspective checking, the app includes translationese detection and extractive summarization. The detector flags unnatural or machine-translated patterns — particularly Chinese-to-Korean or English-to-Korean — so users can better judge linguistic reliability. For summarization, I pretrained KoELECTRA and reached an 80% F1 score on extractive summarization, letting users grasp the core of long articles without losing nuance.
