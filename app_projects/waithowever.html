Wait, However

I re-invent the Fact-Checker

LLM based Fact-Checker

2025.02 –

Wait However is a new kind of fact-checking tool—one that doesn’t just confirm or deny what’s in an article, but actively identifies what’s missing.

Built on large language models (LLMs), Wait However helps readers confront bias, recognize blind spots, and see the other side of sensitive or complex issues. It highlights alternative viewpoints, uncovers underrepresented angles, and surfaces facts or counterarguments that the original article may have ignored.
The goal isn’t to tell users what to believe—it's to empower them to become more democratic, critical consumers of information.

Using the app is simple: just paste the URL of any article you’re reading. Behind the scenes, the AI analyzes the content, contextualizes it with broader knowledge, and returns thoughtful prompts, opposing perspectives, and relevant facts that may shift how you understand the topic.

Wait However is more than a concept—it’s a recognized project that has received awards from competitions including KAIST and other national programs. It's also a showcase of how AI can be used responsibly, not just for generation, but for deeper engagement and accountability in the information ecosystem.

In addition to perspective checking, Wait, However also includes translationese detection and extractive summarization features. The translationese detector flags unnatural or machine-translated patterns—particularly those stemming from Chinese-to-Korean or English-to-Korean translations—so users can better judge the linguistic reliability of the content they read. For summarization, I pretrained the KoELECTRA model, achieving an impressive 80% F1 score in extractive summarization tasks, allowing users to quickly grasp the core of lengthy articles without losing nuance.
